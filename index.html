OnSight: Digital Forensic Evidence Identification Assistant

The Offline-First, AI-Augmented Intelligence for Forensic First Responders

A mobile, offline-first forensic support system that bridges the critical gap in the identification phase of digital investigations. Combining Computer Vision, Signal Analysis, and Rule-Based Knowledge Graphs, OnSight helps investigators identify, prioritize, and legally preserve digital evidence at the scene‚Äîensuring no data is left behind and every action is legally defensible.

View on GitHub Learn More

Problem Statement

Modern digital crime scenes contain numerous potential evidence sources, yet investigators often rely on memory or generic checklists during the critical identification phase‚Äîleading to costly omission errors and incomplete investigations.

Current Challenges

  • Multiple device types (phones, IoT, cloud, wearables)
  • Memory-based decision making prone to errors
  • Generic checklists lack context awareness
  • No structured reasoning or prioritization
  • Inconsistent documentation practices

Research Gap

Existing forensic tools focus on collection and analysis phases, but overlook the identification stage where investigators must decide what evidence exists before any technical work begins. This gap results in missed evidence and incomplete investigations.

Innovation

OnSight elevates beyond simple checklists by integrating AR Evidence Overlay, Silent Sniffer (spectrum analysis), Volatility Countdown (triage engine), Legal Compass (jurisdictional AI), and Chain of Genesis (integrity ledger)‚Äîtransforming every first responder into an expert forensic examiner.

Impact

OnSight is a force multiplier for law enforcement‚Äîdigitizing the expertise of senior forensic examiners and placing it in the pocket of every first responder. It standardizes identification, reduces critical errors, and ensures collected evidence is comprehensive, volatile-aware, and legally admissible.

Advanced Features: The "Perfect App" Integration

OnSight elevates from a simple checklist to a professional-grade tool through five cutting-edge technologies:

üîç

AR Evidence Overlay

Augmented Reality scanning uses real-time object detection (YOLO/EfficientDet) to draw bounding boxes around potential evidence in the camera viewfinder‚Äîhighlighting routers, consoles, or even sticky notes with passwords for exhaustive visual discovery.

üì°

"Silent Sniffer" Spectrum Analysis

Wi-Fi & Bluetooth detection leverages device antennas to scan for invisible, active signals‚Äîidentifying hidden devices like Raspberry Pis inside walls, dropped AirTags, or concealed smart cameras with signal strength alerts.

"Volatility Countdown" Triage Engine

RFC 3227 Order of Volatility automatically ranks evidence by urgency‚Äînot proximity. Critical items like RAM or active cloud sessions are flagged "Red" (Immediate Action Required) to prevent data loss from battery death or timeouts.

"Legal Compass" Jurisdictional AI

Compliance engine cross-references crime type with seizure rules‚Äîproactively warning against overreach (e.g., "Do not seize personal devices without specific warrant") to prevent evidence dismissal in court.

"Chain of Genesis" Integrity Ledger

Cryptographic SHA-256 hashing of every photo, note, and action at capture moment‚Äîcreating an immutable audit trail from "Second Zero" that mathematically proves scene data was never altered, ensuring bulletproof chain of custody.

Context-Aware Recommendations

Specific, explainable instructions instead of generic advice‚Äî"Seize Laptop immediately; high risk of remote wipe. Use Faraday Bag. Check screen for active cloud sessions before closing."
